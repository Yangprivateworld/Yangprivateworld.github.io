Berkeley in California

I lived in a flat near downtown Berkeley, adjacent to the BART station. So it's very convenient for my great march around the Bay area. I rented a single room, so it felt like a 'room of one's own' as Woolf described. Beyond the window pane lies a tapestry of coastline with mountains rising in the distance, and at every dusk, sunset glow emanates, stiring a gentle melancholy within me, which is a common feeling as a petty bourgeois bogemian youth. Downtown

There are only few tall buildings in Berkeley, downtown Berkeley bart station and its vicinity are the most bustling urben area in this small town. But you can't regard the Berkeley town as some remote and dilapidated place, instead, it's a very neat and orderly community(at least cleanner than most places in the Bay area). There are some self-talking vagrants in the street, but they are not hazardous and only try to get some sunbathing in my eyes. Here is the picture of a nearby corssroads.
